Types of Peonies

  • Woodland
  • Tree
  • Herbaceous
  • Intersectional

Description

  • As can be expected from their name, woodland peonies enjoy living amongst trees because of the shade and protection afforded them, by their giant companions.
  • True tree peonies are generally from China or Japan, and are characterized by their exceptional growth and often large blooms. Many tree peonies for purchase are grafts of these peonies.
  • Herbaceous peonies are the most common peony type and the ones you are most likely to see.
  • Intersecional peonies are a hybrid between tree and herbaceous peonies.

Specifics

  • Less common, also generally smaller in stature and flower size.
  • Often 2x the price of an herbaceous peony, these peonies can have flowers the size of dinner plates. In China these peonies can grow into small trees, but in colder climates they will likely remain small.
  • These peonies have unbelievable diversity and come in many different bloom types.
  • These peonies are prolific like tree peonies, with extra large flowers, but die back in winter like herbaceous peonies. They are also called Itoh, after the japanese man who bred them.
